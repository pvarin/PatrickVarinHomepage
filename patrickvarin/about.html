    So what's this site about anyway?

    Hey there! Welcome to my site! This site began as an attempt to teach myself the basics of web programming: HTML, CSS and JavaScript. For some reason in the process I decided that it would be a good idea to post my life on the internet for everyone to see. The result is this site, designed for the world's peering eyes. Come on in, enjoy! Learn what I'm all about.

    School

    I am a second semester sophomore at Olin College, enjoying every minute of it. I am doing my best to masquarade as a Physics major among Engineering majors. Alas, at an engineering school of ~320 the available degrees are limited to...well...engineering, which is why I took advantage of Olin's innovative nature and designed my own major, "Engineering with a concentration in Physics" (E:Physics for short), that satisfies all of the degree requirements of any other Physics degree. Conveniently enough Olin students can cross-register with Wellesley College and Brandeis University, both of which do offer physics degrees. Here I can fill the degree requirements that Olin doesn't offer.

    My interest in physics (and math, by association) probably stems from my love for theory. Theory can be so beautiful at times! There is little in the academic world that is more beautiful in its simplicity than Maxwell's Equations or Einstein's Theory of Relativity. I'll admit that the Standard Model of particle physics could use some cleaning up at the moment, but I have faith that we will come up with something.

    In addition to physics, I am enthralled by every aspect of my studies at Olin. This small community has drawn me into many a crazy project, and allowed me to cultivate academic interests unrelated to physics: designing electrical and mechanical systems, programming, simulating, building. Engineering gives me an opportunity to manipulate the world, while physics provides a framework to understand it. I have found that there are few sensations more incredible then conceiving an idea and laboriously bringing it into fruition. Olin has given me the incredible opportunity to mate my love of theory with the ability to tinker, create and invent!
